Dictionary
Abbreviation
Meaning
Simple definition
Pt
Patient or Prothrombin Time depending on context
In charting, Pt often means patient. In coagulation labs, PT means prothrombin time.
PTT
Partial Thromboplastin Time
A blood clotting test that checks part of the clotting pathway.
aPTT
Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time
A more standardized version of PTT; often used to monitor heparin therapy.
D-Dimer
D-dimer test
Checks for clot breakdown products; can help evaluate possible blood clots.
CMP
Comprehensive Metabolic Panel
Blood test panel checking electrolytes, kidney function, liver function, glucose, and proteins.
BMP
Basic Metabolic Panel
Smaller panel checking electrolytes, kidney function, glucose, calcium, etc.
HCG
Human Chorionic Gonadotropin
Pregnancy hormone test.
CBC
Complete Blood Count
Measures red cells, white cells, hemoglobin, hematocrit, and platelets.
Hematocrit
Hct
Percentage of blood made up of red blood cells.
Hemoglobin
Hgb / Hb
Protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen.
Platelets
Plt
Cell fragments that help blood clot.
ESR
Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate
Measures inflammation by seeing how fast red blood cells settle.
HGA1C / HbA1c
Hemoglobin A1c
Shows average blood sugar over about 2-3 months.
OGTT
Oral Glucose Tolerance Test
Checks how the body handles sugar after drinking glucose.
EDTA
Ethylenediaminetetraacetic Acid
Anticoagulant in lavender/purple-top tubes; prevents clotting.
SPS
Sodium Polyanethol Sulfonate
Anticoagulant used mainly in blood culture bottles/tubes; helps preserve bacteria for testing.
